01 / The Formula

What makes a sitcom
actually great?

Great sitcoms aren't accidents. They follow a formula — then break it in just the right ways.

  1. Unforgettable Characters
    • One straight-man anchor (Jim, Leslie, Jerry)
    • One chaotic wildcard (Dwight, Tom, April)
    • A supporting cast that steals every scene
  2. A Contained World
    • A workplace, apartment, or neighborhood hub
    • Recurring locations that feel like home
    • Rules the characters constantly bend and break
  3. Comedic Timing
    • The pause before the punchline
    • Callbacks to earlier in the episode
    • Running gags that span entire seasons
  4. Just Enough Heart
    • One earned emotional moment per episode
    • Character growth that feels organic
    • Relationships the audience genuinely roots for

The golden age of TV comedy — watched on couches everywhere.

02 / The Greats

The Mount Rushmore
of Sitcoms

These shows didn't just entertain — they defined eras of comedy and set standards every sitcom since has been measured against.

THE OFFICE

The Office (2005–2013)

Michael Scott's Dunder Mifflin is the greatest comedic workplace ever put to screen. The mockumentary format turned awkward silence into an art form.

📺 NBC ⭐ 9.0 / 10 🏆 5× Emmy Winner
SEINFELD

Seinfeld (1989–1998)

A show about nothing that somehow said everything about the mundane absurdity of modern life. Kramer's entrances alone deserve an Emmy.

📺 NBC ⭐ 8.9 / 10 🏆 10× Emmy Winner
PARKS & REC

Parks & Recreation (2009–2015)

Started shaky, became transcendent. Leslie Knope's relentless optimism and Ron Swanson's deadpan made for one of TV's best ensembles ever assembled.

📺 NBC ⭐ 8.6 / 10 🏆 Peabody Award
ARRESTED DEV.

Arrested Development (2003–2019)

The most densely layered comedy ever made. Jokes planted three seasons earlier pay off in a single throwaway line. Always requires a rewatch.

📺 Fox / Netflix ⭐ 8.7 / 10 🏆 6× Emmy Winner
